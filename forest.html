Forestry in India is a significant rural industry and a major environmental resource. India is one of the ten most forest-rich countries of the world.

India is home to probably the best timberlands on the planet. From rich green pine and deodar trees to different untamed life going from tigers to elephants, India's woodlands are home to probably the most stunning living species. An excursion in the midst of one of these woodlands can end up being a restoring and enhancing experience. Not exclusively will it cause you to feel nearer to nature, however it will likewise give you a superior comprehension of your nation and the creatures that dwell in it.

SUNDARBANS,WEST BENGAL

South 24 Parganas and North 24 Parganas District,West bengal,India.1869





The Sundarbans are known to be home to the white tiger (a variation of the Royal Bengal tiger). The huge Sundarbans National park is important for the Sundarbans delta which covers near 10,000 square kilometers and is the biggest deltas on the planet. The rich green mangrove woodland is a tiger save and a biosphere hold. Countless Sundari trees spread over the backwoods which likewise houses fishing felines, panther felines, macaques, wild pig, Indian dim mongoose, fox, wilderness feline, flying fox, pangolin, and chita.
