When considering the best watersports destinations in India, Goa is likely to come to mind first. This isn't surprising as watersports have become hugely popular there, now that they're no longer only being offered by luxury hotels.

However, there are many other unlikely destinations all over the country where watersports are available. Due to their dramatic backdrops, they're watersports with a twist! Think river rafting past remote Buddhist monasteries and kayaking through tropical palm-lined canals. It's sure to be an experience of a lifetime.

SUNDARBANS,WEST BENGAL

South 24 Parganas and North 24 Parganas District,West bengal,India.1869

The Sundarbans are known to be home to the white tiger (a variation of the Royal Bengal tiger). The huge Sundarbans National park is important for the Sundarbans delta which covers near 10,000 square kilometers and is the biggest deltas on the planet. The rich green mangrove woodland is a tiger save and a biosphere hold. Countless Sundari trees spread over the backwoods which likewise houses fishing felines, panther felines, macaques, wild pig, Indian dim mongoose, fox, wilderness feline, flying fox, pangolin, and chita.

Restroom Available. The daytime temperature reaches
29 c and goes to dip to 17 c at night.
Food is available. Appox. 8 hours to enjoy.
